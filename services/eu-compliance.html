Challenge

Regulatory Tsunami: EUDR, CSRD, and Taxonomy

The European Union's regulatory landscape for deforestation-free supply chains has shifted from voluntary to mandatory with the EUDR (2023/1115). Companies face overlapping obligations across EUDR due diligence, CSRD sustainability reporting, and EU Taxonomy alignment.

Our client sourced 7 commodity categories from 12 countries across Latin America, Africa, and Southeast Asia. The EUDR's traceability and geolocation requirements represented a complete overhaul of their procurement information systems.

We aligned the compliance program with four interlocking frameworks: EUDR (Regulation 2023/1115)[1], the CSRD (Directive 2022/2464)[3], the EU Taxonomy (Regulation 2020/852)[4], and ISO 17021-1 certification standards.

Methodology

A Six-Phase Compliance Architecture

Our methodology transforms regulatory complexity into operational workflows and auditable documentation.

1. Regulatory Gap Analysis → 2. Supply Chain Mapping → 3. Risk Assessment → 4. Information System Design → 5. Due Diligence Protocol → 6. Documentation Package

Regulatory Gap Analysis

Mapped client obligations across EUDR Articles 8–12, CSRD ESRS E1/E4, and Taxonomy DNSH criteria, identifying 14 compliance gaps.

Supply Chain Mapping

Traced 234 suppliers across 7 commodity categories to plot-level geolocation using FAO FRA 2020 and WRI Global Forest Watch data.

Information System Design

Built a blockchain-anchored traceability database with automated EUDR Article 9 reference number generation.

Due Diligence Protocol

Designed a risk-based due diligence protocol aligned with ISO 17021-1 management system certification requirements.

📁

Documentation Package

Compiled a complete audit-ready documentation package including satellite evidence, supplier declarations, and risk reports.
