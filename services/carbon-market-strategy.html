Challenge

Integrity in the Voluntary Carbon Market

The voluntary carbon market faces a credibility crisis. Studies have documented systematic over-crediting, weak baseline scenarios, and inadequate leakage accounting. Our client needed a project design that would withstand scrutiny.

Our client sought to develop a 45,000-hectare REDD+ project in the Brazilian Amazon with a target of 12.5 Mt CO₂e over 30 years. The project needed to align with Verra VCS methodology VM0047, Gold Standard requirements, and the new ICVCM Core Carbon Principles[4].

We applied Angelsen et al.'s (2017) results-based payment framework[1] and addressed the over-crediting critiques raised by West et al. (2020)[2] and Badgley et al. (2021)[3] by designing a conservative baseline with robust uncertainty quantification.

⚠️

Market Integrity Risks

West et al. (2020) found that forest-based carbon offsets may not deliver promised climate benefits without stronger MRV. Badgley et al. (2021) documented systematic over-crediting in California's forest offset program.

🛡️

Our Response

Conservative baseline (10th percentile reference region), 3.2% leakage buffer, 20% uncertainty deduction, and full ICVCM CCP alignment.

Methodology

A Seven-Phase MRV Architecture

From baseline scenario to verified carbon credit issuance, every phase is designed for transparency, conservativeness, and auditability.

1. Baseline Scenario → 2. Project Scenario → 3. Leakage Assessment → 4. Uncertainty Quantification → 5. Validation → 6. Verification → 7. Issuance
📊

Baseline Scenario

Historical deforestation trend (2008–2022) projected forward using reference region data at the 10th percentile to ensure conservativeness.

🌳

Project Scenario

Activity data from forest patrols, community monitoring, and remote sensing-based disturbance detection.

🔀

Leakage Assessment

Market leakage (1.8%) and activity-shifting leakage (1.4%) quantified and deducted from net credits.

📐

Uncertainty Quantification

Combined uncertainty propagated through Monte Carlo simulation; 20% deduction applied to net GHG benefits.

✅

Validation

Project design document validated by a VVB accredited under the VCS Program and Gold Standard.

🔍

Verification & Issuance

Annual monitoring reports verified by a VVB; credits issued only after successful verification.
