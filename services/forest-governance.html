Client: Anonymous Cameroonian Community Forest

Multi-community forest governance initiative in the Congo Basin | 2022–2024

Challenge

Complex Tenure, Competing Claims

Community forests in Cameroon face overlapping statutory, customary, and project-level land claims. Without documented rights and participatory governance structures, these forests remain vulnerable to external encroachment and elite capture.

Our client faced a governance vacuum: eight contiguous community forests lacked formal management plans, boundary demarcation, and mechanisms for equitable benefit sharing. Indigenous Bantu and Baka communities held customary use rights that were never formally mapped or recognized under national legislation.

Drawing on Ostrom's institutional analysis framework[1] and Agrawal & Gibson's community-level critique[2], we designed a participatory governance system that links local institutions with national REDD+ frameworks.

🌿

Key Constraints

• No written management plans (50% of community forests nationally)
• Weak boundary enforcement — 23% boundary violations recorded
• Elite capture risk in benefit-sharing committees
• Limited integration with national REDD+ MRV systems

Methodology

A Six-Phase Institutional Approach

Every community forest governance engagement follows a rigorous, participatory methodology grounded in commons theory.

1. Institutional Mapping → 2. Stakeholder Analysis → 3. Rights Documentation → 4. Participatory Mapping → 5. Capacity Assessment → 6. Action Plan
📋

Institutional Mapping

Documented 47 formal and informal institutions governing forest access across 8 community forests, using Ostrom's Institutional Analysis and Development (IAD) framework.

🤝

Stakeholder Analysis

Mapped 156 stakeholders across five actor categories (state, community, private sector, NGO, donor) using power-interest matrix analysis.

📜

Rights Documentation

Compiled statutory and customary rights inventories, cross-referencing national forestry law (1994/1995) with community-level oral agreements.

🗺️

Participatory Mapping

Co-produced 1:50,000 community forest boundary maps with 3,400 households using GPS, drone imagery, and sketch mapping workshops.

📊

Capacity Assessment

Evaluated organizational capacity across 12 domains using a customized scorecard aligned with RRI's tenure assessment framework.

📅

Action Plan

Delivered 5-year community forest management plans with annual milestones, budget allocations, and monitoring indicators.
