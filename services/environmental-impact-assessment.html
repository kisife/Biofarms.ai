Cameroonian Forest Management Concession

12,500-hectare tropical forest concession requiring full Environmental Impact Assessment under Law No. 96/12 and IFC Performance Standards, including biodiversity, hydrological, and socio-economic baseline studies.

Challenge

Assessing Multi-Dimensional Forest Impacts

Tropical forest concessions in Cameroon face complex environmental challenges: biodiversity hotspots, watershed sensitivity, indigenous land use, and carbon stock implications. The assessment required integration of national law (Law 96/12), international standards (IFC, ISO 14001), and best-practice methodologies from Glasson et al.

  • Multi-dimensional impact assessment across 8 environmental components
  • Compliance with Cameroon Law No. 96/12 and IFC Performance Standards
  • Integration of biodiversity data (IUCN Red List, WDPA)
  • Baseline data gaps and remote access constraints
  • Requirement for defensible 5-year monitoring protocol

Methodology

Our Assessment Framework

01

Scoping

Regulatory scoping under Law 96/12 and IFC PS1, identifying 8 environmental components and defining spatial and temporal boundaries of the assessment.

02

Baseline Studies

Field-based baseline data collection supplemented by WRI Global Forest Watch, IUCN Red List, and World Database on Protected Areas for biodiversity and forest cover context.

03

Impact Prediction

Quantitative impact modeling using GIS overlay analysis, habitat equivalency analysis, and hydrological modeling for predicted water quality and quantity changes.

04

Mitigation Design

Mitigation hierarchy application (avoid, minimize, restore, offset) producing 23 specific mitigation measures with quantified performance targets and implementation schedules.

05

Management Plan

Environmental and Social Management Plan (ESMP) with clear responsibilities, budgets, and timelines aligned with ISO 14001:2015 continual improvement framework.

06

Monitoring Protocol

5-year monitoring plan with 47 indicators, sampling frequencies, and trigger thresholds for adaptive management responses, following Glasson et al. monitoring best practices.

07

Public Consultation

Structured public disclosure and consultation process in compliance with Cameroonian EIA regulations and IFC stakeholder engagement requirements.

Key Finding

The EIA identified 3 high-significance impacts (biodiversity habitat loss, watercourse sedimentation, and community forest access disruption) and 5 moderate-significance impacts. The 23 mitigation measures were designed to reduce all high-significance impacts to moderate levels post-implementation, with zero-net-loss commitment for critical habitat. The 5-year monitoring plan includes 47 indicators with quarterly reporting to the Ministry of Environment and IFC compliance monitoring.
