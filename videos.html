From Data to Decisions

AI-generated short films that transform environmental data into compelling narratives — reaching the decision centers that shape our planet's future.

Data Alone Does Not Move Mountains. Stories Do.

Every satellite image, every forest plot, every carbon calculation tells a story. But the data — the pixels, the coordinates, the tonnes of CO2 — never reaches the people who can act on it. That is the gap I bridge.

I do not just collect and analyze environmental data. I craft the narrative that transforms a deforestation statistic into a call to action. I turn a satellite classification into a visual story that lands in boardrooms, government offices, and donor conferences. The same forest that the model misclassified becomes a character in a story that pushes decision-makers to fund, protect, and act.

This is not content creation for its own sake. It is strategic storytelling — using AI-generated video to bring field-verified data to life, because the forest that is not seen is the forest that is not saved. When a policymaker in Geneva watches a short film of the Bakossi landscape, they do not just see coordinates. They see the community, the traditional councils, the centuries of stewardship. They feel the urgency. And they act.
